where are we — shows the New Jersey municipality you are technically in, resolved against the state's official boundary polygons (NJOGIS "Municipal Boundaries of NJ", all 564 municipalities), and keeps a list of every town you've visited — with the municipality type, 2020 Census population, and land area from the same state layer. The zip line is your position tested against US Census ZCTA polygons: a close approximation of postal ZIP areas, not USPS gospel, shown with the postal place name (e.g. 07438 "Oak Ridge"), which in NJ often differs from the actual municipality. The school lines come from NJOGIS's financial-responsibility district boundaries (the district legally responsible for a resident student — reviewed annually with NJDOE) joined to the NJDOE public-school directory by district code; where an elementary and a regional high-school district differ, both are shown. "None on file" usually means a send/receive district whose students attend another district's high school by agreement. Multi-high-school townships list their schools — which one a given street attends is an attendance-zone question no public statewide layer answers. Population and area show the municipality's statewide rank among the 564, computed from the same layer, using the figures as the state publishes them. Legislative and congressional district layers are wired into the code but currently switched off. The map follows your dot; drag it to browse (following stops), and tap the big town name or the "follow" link to snap back. New York and North Carolina are covered with Census TIGERweb boundaries (January 2025 vintage), downloaded lazily the first time you're there: in NY, towns and cities tile the state and villages sit on top of them, so a village address shows both; in NC, most land is outside any municipality, and the app says "unincorporated [county]" because that is the technically correct answer. Outside-NJ readings carry a [census boundary] marker, show area with a statewide rank but no population yet, and skip the NJ-only school lines. The "cache" link in the footer shows exactly what is stored on this device, layer by layer. All layers download once and are cached on this device; after that, answers work offline. The map needs a connection for its base tiles; the boundary lines and your dot do not. Tap the big town name to center the map on yourself; tap a visited row to pan to that town. "days" counts distinct calendar days present. Outside New Jersey it reports coordinates only. Data: NJOGIS / NJGIN Open Data. Map tiles © OpenStreetMap contributors.
